🔋 Battery Technology

⚡ CATL bets sodium-ion will take over grid storage

CATL is deploying 5 GWh of its Tener sodium-ion batteries, citing a 15,000-cycle life and 92% capacity retention at –20°C without a chiller. A cell that can cycle daily for 25–30 years reshapes the levelised-cost maths for grid storage and cuts lithium dependence.

Source: Electrek

⚡ Peak Energy plans a 4-GWh sodium-ion factory in Sacramento

Peak Energy Technologies said on 9 July it will invest up to $71m in a Sacramento plant making sodium-ion storage systems, with capacity of up to 4 GWh a year for utility and commercial projects. It's another sign US-based sodium manufacturing is scaling up.

Source: SodiumBatteryHub

Stellantis road-tests Factorial solid-state cells in a Charger Daytona

Stellantis and Factorial fitted FEST solid-state cells into a Dodge Charger Daytona prototype and began road testing, citing 375 Wh/kg, a 15–90% charge in 18 minutes and operation from –30°C to 45°C. Real-world testing is the step solid-state has to survive before mass production.

Source: Battery-Tech Network

♨️ Heat Pumps

⚡ UK's £9,000 heat-pump grant for off-gas homes starts 21 July

From 21 July the Boiler Upgrade Scheme grant rises 20%, from £7,500 to £9,000, for England & Wales homes off the mains-gas grid that run on oil or LPG boilers. The uplift runs to March 2027 and targets around 1.7 million households where heat pumps make the strongest economic case.

Source: pv magazine

UK installs hit a record 125,037 in 2025 — but growth is lagging target

Heat-pump installations reached a record 125,037 last year, yet 27% growth trails the ~33% compound rate needed to hit 450,000 a year by 2030. In response, the Clean Heat Market Mechanism now requires boiler makers to sell heat pumps equal to 8% of boiler sales or face penalties.

Source: Juniper Research

Panasonic introduced a residential heat-pump line using natural CO₂ (R744) refrigerant, designed for efficient hot water even in cold weather, with up to COP 6.1. Natural refrigerants sidestep the tightening rules on high-GWP synthetic ones.
