☀️ Solar

⚡ Perovskite-silicon triple-junction cell certified at 30.02%

A perovskite-silicon triple-junction solar cell has reached an independently certified 30.02% efficiency, surpassing the previous 27.1% record and pushing three-junction architectures past a symbolic milestone. It shows how stacking materials to capture more of the spectrum keeps unlocking headroom that plain silicon can't reach.

Source: TechXplore

China reclaims commercial-scale efficiency crown

Trina Solar reported a new world record for commercial-format modules, hitting 29.2% conversion efficiency and 907W of output using perovskite-silicon tandem technology. Records at production sizes matter more than lab cells because they signal what buyers may actually be able to install within a few years.

Source: OilPrice.com

Commercial perovskite modules settle into the 24–29% range

Market trackers now place shipping perovskite tandem modules in the 24–29% band, with 30%+ commercial modules targeted for 2027–2028 as manufacturing scales. Full utility-scale confidence still waits on multi-year field durability data.

Source: Fluxim

🔋 Battery Technology

⚡ Low-cost additive opens path to safe all-solid-state sodium batteries

A National University of Singapore team led by Palani Balaya says a single inexpensive additive tames the safety problems that have dogged solid-state sodium cells, giving a scalable route for both grid storage and EVs. Sodium is roughly a thousand times more abundant than lithium, so cheaper, safer chemistry could ease supply-chain pressure.

Source: TechXplore

GM backs sodium-ion cells built for the grid

GM is developing next-generation sodium-ion cells purpose-built for grid-scale storage, partnering with Peak Energy and adding a strategic investment. It's a notable signal that a major automaker sees sodium chemistry as a serious stationary-storage play, not just an EV hedge.

Source: GM News

CATL sodium storage system heads toward 1 GWh shipped

CATL's TENER sodium energy-storage system, launched in Munich, is expected to reach cumulative shipments of 1 GWh by the end of 2026, with the broader sodium-ion market projected to grow from ~70 GWh today to roughly 400 GWh by 2030. Commentators note sodium cells are already nearing lithium-ion cost parity.

Source: Energy Storage News

💨 Wind

⚡ World's first CO₂-reduced-steel, recyclable-blade turbine installed

RWE has installed the world's first offshore turbine combining a CO₂-reduced steel tower with recyclable rotor blades, at the 1.1 GW Thor wind farm off Denmark. All 72 turbines (up to 15 MW each) are due for installation by the end of 2026, a step toward cutting the embodied carbon of wind hardware itself.

Source: RWE

UK opens CfD Allocation Round 8 in July

The UK confirmed its Contracts for Difference Allocation Round 8 opens this month, with up to 18 offshore wind projects potentially eligible to compete. The auction is a key test of how much new offshore capacity Britain can lock in at a time when the sector faces cost pressure.

Source: Wood Mackenzie

US offshore inches forward as Revolution Wind nears commissioning

Ørsted's Revolution Wind is expected to reach full commercial operation in the second half of 2026, while Sunrise Wind has slipped to the second half of 2027. Progress continues even as the US industry navigates a hostile federal backdrop.

Source: The New Bedford Light

♨️ Heat Pumps

⚡ Five New England states launch $450M heat-pump accelerator

Connecticut, Maine, Massachusetts, New Hampshire and Rhode Island have jointly launched a $450 million New England Heat Pump Accelerator, funded through federal Climate Pollution Reduction Grants. It's a notable state-led backstop as federal support recedes.

Source: Harvest Thermal

Federal air-source credit gone; states and geothermal fill the gap

The federal Section 25C tax credit for air-source heat pumps expired after 31 December 2025, so 2026 installs lean on state rebates (HEEHRA, HOMES) and utility programs; geothermal keeps its 30% federal credit through 2032. Buyers now have to navigate a patchwork rather than a single national incentive.

Source: ACDirect

Heat pumps keep outselling gas furnaces

Industry data shows heat pumps outsold gas furnaces nationally for a second straight year, with 2026 described as an inflection point where installs outpace fossil replacements on economics alone. Cold-climate models now hold performance down to roughly −20°F / −25°C.

Source: Euromonitor

💧 Hydro

⚡ World Bank approves $265M for Morocco pumped-storage project

The World Bank board approved $265 million on 1 July to support the Ifahsa Pumped Hydropower Storage Project near Chefchaouen — one of Africa's most significant pumped-storage schemes, designed as a giant rechargeable battery for Morocco's grid. It's a marquee example of pumped hydro anchoring renewables in an emerging market.

Source: World Bank

Global pumped storage passes 200 GW — the 'year of the water battery'

The World Hydropower Outlook reports global hydropower capacity reached 1,469 GW in 2025 after 28 GW of additions, including a record 11.6 GW of pumped storage that pushed the global pumped fleet past 200 GW for the first time. Long-duration storage is scaling just as variable renewables need it most.

Source: International Hydropower Association

Cruachan and Tehri upgrades bring more flexible capacity online

Refurbished machines at Scotland's Cruachan pumped-storage plant are being commissioned across 2026–2027, while India's Tehri project — its first variable-speed pumped storage — completes its four 250 MW reversible units. Both add the fast-ramping flexibility grids increasingly rely on.

Source: International Water Power
