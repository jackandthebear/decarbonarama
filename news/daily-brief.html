Source: SodiumBatteryHub

Bottom line

Today's throughline is that storage is where the money and momentum are: Spain and India are pouring capacity into pumped hydro, sodium-ion is going from lab to factory floor in Sacramento, and even wave power just landed a €40m cheque. On the generation side, solar tandem cells keep breaking records and fusion pulled in a record $4.48bn despite still being years from the grid. The awkward counterpoint is policy: the US federal heat-pump credit has lapsed, leaving a patchwork of state rebates, and offshore wind is advancing on engineering momentum more than political tailwinds. The technology is ready and getting cheaper — the open question everywhere is whether policy and grid connections keep pace.
