☀️ Solar

⚡ LONGi pushes perovskite-silicon tandem to 34.85% certified efficiency

LONGi has logged an NREL-certified world record of 34.85% for a perovskite-silicon tandem cell, well beyond the practical ceiling of plain crystalline silicon. It underscores how fast tandem technology is closing the gap between the lab and the rooftop.

Source: Interesting Engineering

Trina sets commercial-scale tandem record at 29.2% and 907W

Trina Solar reported a new commercial-scale mark of 29.2% conversion efficiency and 907 watts of output from a perovskite-silicon tandem module. Commercial-scale numbers matter more than lab cells because they signal what can actually ship at volume.

Source: OilPrice.com

Tandem financing still the bottleneck, not the physics

Analysts note utility-scale perovskite warranties sit at 10–20 years versus 25–30 for silicon, and the lack of 20-year field data limits traditional debt financing. Mainstream residential tandem availability is more likely a 2027–2028 story as manufacturing and bankability catch up.

Source: Energy Solutions Intelligence

Solid-state sodium battery targets cheaper, safer grid storage

Researchers reported an all-solid-state sodium battery designed to cut grid storage costs and reduce lithium dependence, tackling the safety weaknesses that have dogged earlier sodium cells. With sodium roughly a thousand times more abundant than lithium, the chemistry is attractive where cost and supply security dominate.

♨️ Heat Pumps

⚡ UK lifts Boiler Upgrade Scheme grant to £9,000 for oil-heated homes from 21 July

From 21 July the Boiler Upgrade Scheme grant rises 20%, from £7,500 to £9,000, to help rural households swap heating oil for a heat pump; leaflets are landing on 200,000 eligible doormats. The uplift runs until 31 March 2027 and targets off-grid homes exposed to volatile oil prices without Ofgem price-cap protection.

Source: GOV.UK

R290 propane heat pumps debut at InstallerSHOW 2026

Major manufacturers unveiled R290 (propane) models at InstallerSHOW at the NEC Birmingham this week, ahead of rules taking effect 1 January 2027. From that date any new split heat pump or AC unit rated 12kW or below must use a refrigerant with a global warming potential under 150 — a bar R290's GWP of 3 clears easily.

Source: Grant Store
