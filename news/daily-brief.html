☀️ Solar

⚡ LONGi breaks the tandem cell record again — 35.5%

LONGi unveiled a certified 35.5% efficiency for its crystalline silicon-perovskite tandem cell, presented on 14 July and verified by the European Solar Test Installation. That extends a run that has taken the company from 34.85% to 35.5% in under a year, pushing well past silicon's practical ceiling.

Source: SolarQuarter

Perovskite tandems edge toward the mainstream

Coverage this week frames 2026 as the "early adopter" year for perovskite tandems, with LONGi reporting module efficiencies above 31% at near-industrial dimensions. Mass-market rooftop availability is still pencilled in for 2027–28 as manufacturing scales and long-term field data accumulates.

Source: CleanTechnica

🔋 Battery Technology

⚡ Peak Energy to build first US sodium-ion gigafactory

Peak Energy announced on 9 July it will invest up to $71m in a Sacramento plant it bills as the first sodium-ion manufacturing site of its kind in the US, producing up to 4 GWh of storage systems a year. Production and shipments are slated to begin in Q1 2027.

Source: SodiumBatteryHub

Solid-state cells hit the road in a Dodge Charger

Stellantis and Factorial fitted FEST solid-state cells into a Charger Daytona prototype and began road testing, citing 375 Wh/kg, a 15–90% charge in 18 minutes, and operation from −30°C to 45°C. It is one of the first solid-state demonstrations in a full production-platform vehicle.

Source: Battery-Tech Network

Sodium-ion moves from lab to mass market

Changan is preparing the Nevo A06 as one of the first mass-produced sodium-ion EVs, with CATL supplying a second-generation pack. Morgan Stanley now expects sodium-ion to reach 20% of global battery deployment by 2030, rising to 37% by 2035.

Source: SodiumBatteryHub

♨️ Heat Pumps

⚡ UK heat pump grant rises to £9,000 for oil & LPG homes

The Boiler Upgrade Scheme grant increases to £9,000 for off-gas-grid homes on oil or LPG from 21 July — a £1,500 uplift and the scheme's largest single increase, aimed at the UK's 1.7 million off-grid households. The 0% VAT relief on heat pumps runs until 31 March 2027.

Source: Homebuilding & Renovating

Record installs, but pace still short of target

UK heat pump installations hit a record 125,037 in 2025, but 27% growth trailed the ~33% compound rate needed to reach 450,000 a year by 2030. Panasonic launched a residential CO₂ air-to-water range claiming up to COP 6.1, and average install costs are running about 10% below 2023 in real terms.

Source: HeatPumpsAndSolar

💧 Hydro

⚡ UK approves three Scottish pumped-storage schemes

Britain has provisionally approved three major pumped storage hydropower projects in Scotland — the first of their kind in over 40 years — to act as large-scale "water batteries" balancing intermittent wind and solar. Cruachan's unit upgrades are also being commissioned across 2026–27.

Source: No2NuclearPower

Global pumped storage passes 200 GW

The World Hydropower Outlook reports global installed hydro capacity reached 1,469 GW in 2025 after 28 GW of additions, including a record 11.6 GW of pumped storage. Cumulative pumped-storage capacity has now crossed 200 GW for the first time, cementing it as the dominant form of large-scale storage.

Source: International Hydropower Association

🌊 Wave & Tidal

⚡ First continental-US wave power PPA signed

The Bonneville Power Administration signed a power purchase agreement to buy up to 20 MWh per hour from Oregon's PacWave facility, spanning 2026–2030 — the first PPA covering wave power in the continental US. PacWave South plans to host its first developers this summer.

Source: Yale E360

CorPower lands €40m for Portuguese wave farm

CorPower Ocean secured a €40m EU Innovation Fund grant to develop VianaWave, a 10 MW pre-commercial wave farm off northern Portugal. A new IRENA cost report projects wave and tidal costs falling to $120–140/MWh at 2 GW of installed capacity, validating the path to commercial scale.

Source: ECO Magazine
