☀️ Solar

⚡ Tandem perovskite passes 30% as market forecast balloons

Fresh 2026 tallies confirm perovskite-silicon tandem cells have cleared the 30% barrier — LONGi holds an NREL-certified 34.85% lab record while Trina's commercial-scale tandem hit 29.2% efficiency and 907 W of output. Analysts now see the perovskite market rising from ~$1.94bn in 2025 to ~$24bn by 2035.

Commercialisation still gated on durability

Oxford PV's first 24.5% modules are shipping to US utility customers, proving the manufacturing lines are real. But pure perovskite still degrades fast under heat, moisture and UV, so full utility-scale rollout is pencilled in for 2027–2029 once 20-year field data exists.

Source: Energy Solutions Intelligence

Source: Battery-Tech Network

The UK's Contracts for Difference AR8 opens this month with up to 18 offshore projects potentially eligible. Meanwhile RWE installed the world's first turbine with a CO2-reduced steel tower and recyclable blades at Denmark's Thor wind farm, and Dogger Bank A finished turbine installation.

♨️ Heat Pumps

⚡ Installations overtake fossil replacements

2026 marks an inflection point: for the first time in many developed markets, annual heat pump installations are outpacing gas boiler and fossil furnace replacements. Cold-climate models now run reliably down to −25°C.

Source: Oxmaint

US states step in as federal credits lapse

With federal air-source heat pump tax credits expired at end-2025 (only geothermal still qualifies), five New England states launched a coordinated $450m Heat Pump Accelerator aiming to fund up to 580,000 residential installs by 2030 and cut 2.5m tonnes of emissions.

Source: Harvest Thermal

Bottom line

Today's theme is scale meeting policy pivots. Perovskite tandems and sodium-ion are graduating from lab to line, offshore wind has cleared 92 GW, and pumped storage just passed 200 GW globally — the storage-and-generation build-out is accelerating on every front. On the policy side the picture is mixed: the UK is opening CfD Round 8 for both offshore wind and SMRs, and the World Bank is bankrolling African pumped hydro, even as US federal heat-pump credits lapse and states rush to fill the gap. Fusion remains the long game, with Commonwealth queuing for a grid connection but real electrons still years out. For UK homeowners the near-term signal is clear: heat pumps have crossed the tipping point, and the funding follows the technologies that already work.
