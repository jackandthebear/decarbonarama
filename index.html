☀️ Solar Panels

Harvest the power of the sun

Modern solar photovoltaic (PV) panels convert sunlight into clean electricity for your home. With payback periods under 8 years and 25-year warranties, solar is the foundation of every green home.

How solar panels work

Solar panels are made up of photovoltaic cells that absorb photons from sunlight and convert them into direct current (DC) electricity. An inverter then transforms this into the alternating current (AC) your home appliances use.

A typical UK home installation of 10–12 panels (3.5–4kW system) will generate around 3,000–4,000 kWh of electricity per year — covering roughly 50–70% of a household's typical usage.

Any surplus electricity can be exported to the grid via the Smart Export Guarantee (SEG), earning you additional income on top of your savings.

🔋 Battery Storage

Store energy. Use it on your terms.

Home battery systems capture the solar energy you generate — or cheap overnight grid electricity — and make it available whenever you need it. Never waste free solar power again.

Why battery storage changes everything

Without a battery, surplus solar energy is exported to the grid for as little as 5–15p/kWh. With a battery, you store that energy and use it in the evening when grid electricity costs 28–35p/kWh — a significant difference.

Batteries also enable "time-of-use" arbitrage — charging from the grid overnight at cheap rates (as low as 7p/kWh on Octopus Agile) and discharging during peak hours. Many customers earn back £400–£600/year from this alone.

And in a power cut, a battery with islanding capability keeps your lights, fridge, and devices running — essential peace of mind.

♨️ Heat Pumps

Ditch gas. Heat smarter.

Air source heat pumps extract warmth from outside air — even at -20°C — and deliver it into your home at 3–4x the energy efficiency of a gas boiler. The future of home heating is here.

How heat pumps work

A heat pump works like a refrigerator in reverse. A refrigerant fluid absorbs heat energy from the outside air, is compressed to raise its temperature, and then transfers that heat to your home's heating and hot water system.

The magic number is the Coefficient of Performance (COP). A good heat pump delivers a COP of 3–4, meaning for every 1kWh of electricity used, 3–4kWh of heat energy is produced. No gas boiler can match this.

Powered by your own solar energy, a heat pump can make your home truly zero-carbon for heating — a major step towards meeting the UK's 2050 net-zero target.

Key Benefits
🌡️

300–400% Efficient

A COP of 3–4 means heat pumps are up to 4x more efficient than even a brand new gas boiler.

Works in All Weathers

🚿

Heating & Hot Water

One system handles all your space heating and domestic hot water needs all year round.

🌬️

Cooling Capability

Many heat pumps can reverse to provide cooling in summer — a growing need in the UK climate.

📉

Lower Running Costs

When paired with solar, many homeowners see heating costs drop by 50–70% vs gas.

⚡ EV & Home Charging

Drive on sunshine. Charge at home.

Pair a smart home EV charger with your solar panels and charge your car using energy you've generated yourself. The combination of solar, battery, and EV charging is the ultimate green home package.

Why home EV charging makes sense

Public charging is getting faster but remains expensive — often 60–80p/kWh. Charging at home from solar costs effectively zero, and overnight on an Octopus Go tariff just 7.5p/kWh. Over 10,000 miles a year, the difference is over £1,000.

Smart chargers go further still. They communicate with your battery, solar inverter, and energy tariff to automatically charge your car at the optimal time — maximising solar use and minimising cost without you lifting a finger.

Vehicle-to-home (V2H) and vehicle-to-grid (V2G) technology is now also available, allowing your EV's battery to power your home during peak electricity pricing periods.

AI-powered energy management systems learn your routines, predict weather patterns, and automatically orchestrate your solar, battery, heat pump, and EV to minimise your bills and maximise your green impact.

The brain of your green home

A green home with multiple technologies — solar, battery, heat pump, EV — is only as smart as the system managing them. AI energy management systems connect all of these devices and make thousands of micro-decisions every day to optimise your home's performance.

Machine learning models analyse your historical usage, live energy prices, weather forecasts, and grid carbon intensity. They then decide: should the battery charge now from solar, or wait for a cloud to pass? Should the EV charge now or in three hours when the wind picks up and prices drop?

The result is a home that continuously gets smarter, cheaper to run, and lower in carbon — without you needing to think about it.
