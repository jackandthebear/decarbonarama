Scheme 1 — England & Wales

Boiler Upgrade Scheme (BUS)

Boiler Upgrade Scheme — standard £7,500
Who: Owner-occupiers and private landlords in England and Wales, replacing an existing fossil fuel or electric heating system with a heat pump or biomass boiler
Technologies: Air source heat pump (£7,500) · Ground source heat pump (£7,500) · Biomass boiler (£5,000, limited to rural properties off mains gas) · Air-to-air heat pump (£2,500)
How it works: Your MCS-certified installer claims the grant and deducts it from your invoice — you never see the money, it directly reduces the price you pay
EPC requirement: A valid EPC within the last 10 years is required. The previous insulation pre-conditions were removed in April 2026 — no minimum energy efficiency rating needed
New builds: Not eligible, except self-builds meeting specific criteria
Runs until: Confirmed through to 2028–2029 as part of the Warm Homes Plan, with funding increasing annually to 2029–30
Open — live now
BUS Oil & LPG Uplift — temporary £9,000
Who: Homes in England and Wales currently heated by heating oil or LPG (off mains gas grid only)
Technologies eligible for uplift: Air source heat pumps and ground source heat pumps only — biomass boilers and air-to-air systems are not eligible for the higher rate
When: Applications expected to open July 2026, subject to formal grant change notice by DESNZ. Runs for current financial year only — applications after it closes revert to £7,500
Why oil and LPG? Around 1.7 million homes in England and Wales rely on oil or LPG. Unlike mains gas users, they have no energy price cap protection and are directly exposed to global commodity markets — including oil price spikes from the ongoing Middle East conflict. Nesta analysis shows the £9,000 grant reduces net installation cost to approximately £3,600 for a detached house
From July 2026 Time-limited — this financial year only

Important change from April 2026: The requirement to have loft and cavity wall insulation in place before applying for the BUS grant was removed. You no longer need to clear insulation recommendations from your EPC before accessing the £7,500 grant. This has opened the scheme to a significantly wider group of homeowners who were previously blocked by the insulation condition.

Scheme 2 — Great Britain

ECO4 — Energy Company Obligation

ECO4 — free insulation, heating & solar for low-income homes £7,000–£20,000+
Who: Households in Great Britain (England, Scotland, Wales) receiving qualifying benefits (Universal Credit, Pension Credit, Income Support, JSA, ESA, Housing Benefit, Child Tax Credit, Working Tax Credit) — or via LA Flex: household income below your council's threshold (typically £31,000–£38,000)
What it covers: Whole-house approach — insulation (loft, cavity wall, solid wall, underfloor), heat pumps, boiler replacement, solar panels, heating controls. The package is determined by what will most improve the property's EPC rating
Cost to you: Free — energy suppliers fund the scheme as an obligation, passed on to all energy bill payers. Social housing tenants should contact their landlord, not apply directly
LA Flex: If you don't receive benefits, check with your local council — they can apply ECO4 funding to households below their income threshold. Exact limits vary from £31,000 to £38,000 depending on household size and council
Status: Extended from March 2026 to 31 December 2026. No successor ECO5 scheme — will be replaced by Warm Homes Plan funding from 2027
Note on quality: A National Audit Office report (October 2025) found significant quality issues with some ECO4 installations, particularly external wall insulation. Use a TrustMark-registered installer and request PAS 2030/2035 compliance
Ends 31 December 2026
Scheme 3 — England

Warm Homes: Local Grant

Warm Homes: Local Grant — up to £30,000 for low-income EPC D-G homes Up to £30,000
Who: England only. Owner-occupiers or private tenants (with landlord consent) with household income below £36,000/yr, OR receiving any qualifying means-tested benefit, AND property EPC rating D to G
Structure: Up to £15,000 for energy performance improvements (insulation, draught-proofing, double/triple glazing, solar PV, batteries) + up to £15,000 for low-carbon heating (heat pumps). Total maximum: £30,000 per property
All fuel types: Unlike previous schemes, the WH:LG covers both on-gas-grid and off-gas-grid homes. Heat pump upgrades are particularly encouraged for oil and coal-heated properties
How to apply: Via your local council — not a national website. 271 councils (97% of eligible authorities) received funding in 2025. Contact your local authority directly or search gov.uk for your council's scheme
Landlords: Can get one property upgraded free. Additional properties require a 50% cost contribution. Landlords must sign a commitment not to raise rents as a result of the upgrades
Runs: April 2025 to March 2028 · £500 million total allocation
Open — apply via local council

The WH:LG is delivered by local councils, not private installers. You should not need to deal with installers directly — the council arranges the works if you qualify. Some councils have already met their Year 1 targets and are holding applications for Year 2 funding from April 2026. Apply early, even if told to wait.

Scheme 4 — UK

The Warm Homes Plan — the overarching framework

Scotland — entirely different system

Scotland: Home Energy Scotland (HES) Grant and Loan

Scotland operates its own home energy scheme through the Scottish Government, separate from all England and Wales schemes. The single point of contact for Scottish homeowners is Home Energy Scotland: 0808 808 2282 (free, Mon–Fri 8am–8pm, Sat 9am–5pm). They act as a gateway to all Scottish energy schemes and will assess which you qualify for in a single call.

Home Energy Scotland Grant and Loan Up to £18,000 grant + £7,500 loan
Who: Scottish homeowners only. You must be the owner-occupier of an existing Scottish property as your primary residence. No means-testing — you do not need to be on benefits to qualify. This is the key difference from England's schemes
Heat pump grant: Up to £7,500 grant towards a heat pump or heat network connection. If the cost is £7,500 or less, the full amount is a grant (non-repayable). Costs above £7,500 can be covered by the optional 0% loan
Energy efficiency grant: Up to 75% of the combined cost of insulation measures, up to a maximum grant of £7,500. The remaining balance can be taken as an interest-free loan
Rural/island uplift: An additional £1,500 on both the heat pump grant and energy efficiency grant for rural, island, and off-gas accessible rural homes as defined by the Scottish Government's Urban Rural Classification. Rural households can therefore claim up to £18,000 in grants alone
0% interest-free loan: Up to £7,500 additional for heating, repayable over up to 10 years with no interest. Can be repaid early with no penalty
Stacking with BUS: Scottish homeowners can combine the HES grant with the UK-wide BUS grant — a £7,500 HES grant + £7,500 BUS grant = £15,000 toward a heat pump installation. With a £12,000 install, this is effectively free
Open — call 0808 808 2282
Warmer Homes Scotland — fuel poverty scheme Free upgrades
Who: Scottish households experiencing fuel poverty — assessed through income, property type, and current energy costs. Delivered through Home Energy Scotland referral
What: Fully funded insulation, heating upgrades, and energy efficiency improvements at no cost to the household
Apply via: Home Energy Scotland (0808 808 2282) who will assess eligibility and refer eligible households
"A Scottish rural homeowner installing a £12,000 heat pump could receive £7,500 HES grant + £1,500 rural uplift + £7,500 BUS grant = £16,500 — covering the full installation cost with money left over."

The ECO4 scheme also operates in Scotland — Scottish households on qualifying benefits or below LA Flex income thresholds can apply through energy suppliers. Scotland therefore has three potential routes running simultaneously: HES Grant/Loan, BUS, and ECO4. Home Energy Scotland will identify which combination applies to your circumstances.

Banks & lenders

Green mortgages, loans and bank schemes

The least-known dimension of home decarbonisation funding is the parallel ecosystem of bank and lender products. There are now 27 unsecured green loan products available in 2025 — up from just nine in 2021 — introduced by lenders ranging from high-street banks like Barclays to specialist lenders like Zopa Bank. These do not require benefit eligibility or EPC rating conditions — they are available to anyone with a mortgage or who can access personal finance.

Discounted rates for purchasing new builds with EPC A/B. £500 cashback for customers who go solar with a certified installer. Also offers a Greener Home Reward of up to £2,000 for existing mortgage holders making qualifying improvements.

£1,000 cashback to mortgage customers who install solar with a certified installer. Available to both Halifax and Lloyds Bank mortgage holders.

The remortgage timing opportunity: An estimated 1.8 million UK fixed-rate mortgages expire in 2026. If your fixed rate is ending, this is the optimal moment to explore a green remortgage — the rate differential between a standard remortgage and a green remortgage product may be sufficient to help fund solar panels, insulation, or a heat pump at lower cost than a separate loan.

The grant landscape allows — and in many cases encourages — combining multiple schemes. Here are the most powerful combinations available in 2026:

Key stacking rules to know: BUS can be combined with HES in Scotland, with WH:LG only in limited circumstances (usually one or the other applies), and with bank lending freely. You cannot claim two whole-house heating systems under any single scheme — only one primary heat source per application. ECO4 and WH:LG cannot both be applied to the same measure in the same property, but may cover different improvements in the same home.

Start here: the fastest route for your situation

You're in Scotland: Call Home Energy Scotland on 0808 808 2282. Free call, expert advisors, they will identify every scheme you qualify for in one conversation. Do this before anything else.

You're in England, on benefits or income below £36,000, EPC D-G: Apply to the Warm Homes: Local Grant via your local council first. Up to £30,000 fully funded. If your council's year 1 allocation is full, register your interest for year 2.

You're in England, on benefits, EPC D-G, urgently need help now: ECO4 is your fastest route while it lasts — contact your energy supplier directly before December 2026.

You're in England or Wales, any income, replacing gas boiler with heat pump: The BUS £7,500 grant is available to you via your MCS-certified installer. If you're on oil or LPG, wait for the July 2026 uplift to £9,000 if your installation is not urgent.

Your mortgage is expiring in 2026: Combine the BUS grant with a green remortgage additional borrowing product. NatWest's 0% additional borrowing (£5,000–£20,000) combined with BUS can fund a solar + heat pump installation with minimal out-of-pocket cost.

The £5 billion Warm Homes Fund — offering zero or low-interest loans for solar, batteries, and heat pumps to all homeowners — has been confirmed but not yet launched. Watch gov.uk/warm-homes-plan for the opening date. This will be the route for households who are above the income threshold for grants but want government-backed finance.
